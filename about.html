Hola, I’m William.

I’m a UX designer and journalist from Medellín. I work at the intersection of design, education, technology, and public value.

This page is a bit different. It’s not only about what I’ve done — it’s about how I think, what shaped me, and why I design the way I do.

Scroll down to read my story.

Where I come from

I started my career in Colombia’s Ministry of Education, designing digital platforms for teachers, students, and families across the country. Working in the public sector shaped my core belief: design is a tool to create access, capacity, and opportunity.

How I think and work

Before becoming a designer, I studied journalism — and that background still influences everything I do. I research deeply, write to clarify my thinking, and treat information architecture as a way to create meaning. I like to simplify complexity without oversimplifying people.

What keeps me curious

I teach sensory thinking at the University of Antioquia. I run a small film project called Alucine. I explore how AI can expand a designer’s analytical range.

Wherever I work, I’m paying attention to perception, narrative, and the ways people make sense of digital spaces.
